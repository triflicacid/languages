Nouns

Overview

Nouns have one of two geners: common and neuter. Nouns are inflected for number, definiteness and can take a genitive suffix:

Noun stem Plural ending Definite article Genitive -s

Articles

Nouns are classified into five declensions named after the indefinite plural suffix. Note that if the noun ends with a vowel and the suffix starts with a vowel, then the original vowel is replaced.

Singular Plural
Common Gender
Indefinite en (suffix)
Definite [den] -en, -n [de] (suffix) + na/a/en*
Neuter Gender
Indefinite ett (suffix)
Definite [det] -et, -t [de] (suffix) + na/a/en*

Notes

  • Square brackets - this article is present before any adjectives/numbers e.g. flaskan (the bottle) vs den nya flaskan (the new bottle).
  • (suffix) - stands in for the declension-dependent suffix.
  • (*) -na is used for declensions 1, 2 and 3, -a is declension 4, and -en is declension 5.

Declensions

  • First declension: common gender, -or. Most nouns end in -a, in which case it is replaced.
  • Second declension: common gender, -ar.
  • Third declension: -er. If the noun ends in a vowel, just add an -r instead.
  • Fourth declension: neuter gender, -n. All nouns in this declension end in a vowel.
  • Fifth declension: (no ending).
