Pronunctiation

Alphabet

Letter English Transliteration Notes
A, a Ah Put down your jaw. A cross between ah and aw.
Å, å Oh With a Swedish "O" pronunctiation. Like cross between "A" and "O".
Ä, ä Aih Like a combination of A and E. Open your mouth wide.
B, b Bee
C, c See Show your teeth
D, d Dee Show your teeth
E, e Ee Show your teeth
F, f Eff
G, g g-ee Like the G in get
H, h Who Like karate
I, i Ee Like "E" in Swedish, but show teeth less. Quite nasaly. Put tongue behind bottom teeth.
J, j Yaye In words, pronounced more like Swedish "I" with a zhe sound.
K, k Ku-wa "Kan" is pronounced like the english but more emphasis on the "a".
L, l Ell
M, m Emm
N, n Enn
O, o Oow/Ew A combination of ow and ew. Form a circle with your mouth.
Ö, ö Oer Combination of O and E. Jaw low.
P, p Pee
Q, q Ku
R, r Air The R is trilled. It is strong in the alphabet or at the beginning of words and weaker elsewhere.
S, s Ess
T, t Tea
U, u Ew/Yew Like "O", but no circled lips. More smooshed.
V, v Vee
W, w W Exactly like the English
X, x Ecs Exactly like the English
Y, y Euu Like ayy. Pucker your lips out.
Z, z Sa-ta
