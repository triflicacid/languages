Articles, Gender & Number

Overview

Articles

Articles include both the definite the (known/specific) and the indefinite a/an (unknown/non-specific) and appear before a noun and are used to mark the identificability of the noun phrase. In Italian, the article must agree with number and grammatical gender, and changes with the noun's starting letter.

Gender

Italian has two grammatical genders: masculine and feminine. Every noun has an associated gender. Pronouns, adjectives and articles must agree in gender with the noun they are describing, so knowing the gender of any one noun is important.

Number

Italian nouns have two numbers: singular (1), and plural (2+). Like gender, number must agreed with words such as adjectives and pronouns.

Gender and Number

The gender of an Italian noun can usually be determined by its final letter, but on other occasions may this may be decieving and one must have to learn the gender.

L'

Masculine or feminine nouns take the article l' when the noun begins with a vowel - a, e, i, o, u - or h. The vowel collapses into the article which binds onto the noun.

Lo

Masculine nouns take the article lo when the noun begins with s+consonant, z, ps, gn or y
