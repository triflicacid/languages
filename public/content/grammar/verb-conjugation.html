Verb Conjugation

Conjuagtion is when a verb is inflected to portray

  • Aspect — how the verb extends over time
  • Gender — indicate the (grammatical) gender of the subject/object
  • Mood — attitude, such as imperative and subjunctive
  • Person — the subject/reciever of the verb
  • Tense — indicate the time reference when the verb occured

Verbs are split into 3 categories which follow a fixed pattern:

  • 1st conjugation: -are
  • 2nd conjugation: -ere
    • This conjugation includes -arre, -orre and -urre as the first e in ere fused to the stem
  • 3rd conjugation: -ire
    • Includes the semi-irregular: infix -isc- between the stem and the ending

Verbs which do not follow some/all of a conjugation's rules are irregular.

Nominal Forms

Present Participle

The Present Participle may be used as an adjective or noun to describe a present continous scenario.

  • stem + -are → stem + -ante
  • stem + -ere → stem + -ente
  • stem + -ire → stem + -ente

For example, thinking in I'm thinking is a present participle, running in running water is a present participle acting as a noun

Past Participle

The Past Participle is a noun which may be used in forming the perfect passive.

  • stem + -are → stem + -ato
  • stem + -ere → stem + -uto
  • stem + -ire → stem + -ito

For example, looked in have you looked?, lost in lost property

The Present Tense

Amare Credere Dormire
Io Amo Credo Dormo
Tu Ami Credi Dormi
Lui/Lei Ama Crede Dorme
Noi Amiamo Crediamo Dormiamo
Voi Amate Credete Dormite
Loro Amano Credono Dormono

Examples

Il ragazzo ama sua madre
The boy loves his mother

Io credo il giornale
I believe the newspaper

The Present Perfect

The present perfect is used for past actions/events which happened once.

It is formed from an auxiliary verb + past participle

Past Participle

  • -are → -ato
  • -ere → -uto
  • -ire → -ito

Auxiliary Verb

Is either avere (to have) or essere (to be). Most verbs take avere, but a few intransitive verbs and all reflexive verbs take essere

Amare Arrivare
Io Ho Amato Sono Arrivato
Tu Hai Amato Sei Arrivato
Lui/Lei Ha Amato È Arrivato
Noi Abbiamo Amato Siamo Arrivato
Voi Avete Amato Siete Arrivato
Loro Hanno Amato Sono Arrivato

Note, for past participles which have essere, the ending -o changes with gender/number of the subject: -o, -i, -a or -e

Examples

Io ho dormito in panchina
I slept on the bench

Le sono io creduto
I believed her

The Imperfect

The imperfect is used repeated past actions/events or for continuing states of affairs.

The endings are formed by removing -re and suffixing -v along with the endings of Present -are verbs, except -iamo which becomes -amo

Amare Credere Dormire
Io Amavo Credevo Dormivo
Tu Amavi Credevi Dormivi
Lui/Lei Amava Credeva Dormiva
Noi Amavamo Credevamo Dormivamo
Voi Amavate Credevate Dormivate
Loro Amavano Credevono Dormivono

Examples

Io dormivo sulle panchine
I used to sleep on benches

Lui credeve in dio
He used to believe in God

The Past Perfect

The Past Perfect is used for activities done prior to another activity.

The Past Perfect is formed the same as the Present Perfect but with the auxiliary verb in the Imperfect

Imperfect Auxiliary Verbs

Avere Essere
Io Avevo Ero
Tu Avevi Eri
Lui/Lei Aveva Era
Noi Avevamo Eravamo
Voi Avevate Eravate
Loro Avevano Erano

Examples

Il ragazzo aveva mangiato del pane
The boy had eaten some bread

The Future

The future tense is used to convey actions that will happen.

It is formed from the infinitive, minus the final e, and with the endings -ò, -ai, -à, -emo, -ete, -anno

  • -are → -ere
  • -nr and -lr simplify to -rr-

Amare Credere Dormire
Io Amerò Crederò Dormirò
Tu Amerai Crederai Dormirai
Lui/Lei Amerà Crederà Dormirà
Noi Ameremo Crederemo Dormiremo
Voi Amerete Crederete Dormirete
Loro Ameranno Crederanno Dormiranno

Examples

Il ragazzo mangierà del pane
The boy will eat some bread

Passerò una buona notte di riposo!
I will have a good night's rest!

The Future Perfect

The future perfect is used to events which will have happened in the future.

It is formed the same as the Present Perfect, but with the auxiliary verb in the Future

Future Auxiliary Verbs

Avere Essere
Io Avrò Sarò
Tu Avrai Sarai
Lui/Lei Avrà Sarà
Noi Avremo Saremo
Voi Avrete Sarete
Loro Avranno Saranno

Examples

Il ragazzo avrà mangiato del pane
The boy will have eaten some bread

Conditional

The Conditional is used for events which are dependant on others occuring, or for questions.

Formed by joining the stem of the Future with -ei, -esti, -ebbe, -emmo, -este, -ebbero

Amare Credere Dormire
Io Amerei Crederei Dormirei
Tu Ameresti Crederesti Dormiresti
Lui/Lei Amerebbe Crederebbe Dormirebbe
Noi Ameremmo Crederemmo Dormiremmo
Noi Amereste Credereste Dormireste
Noi Amerebbero Crederebbero Dormirebbero

The Past Conditional

For events that would or should have occured, or as a prospective past tense.

It is formed the same as the Present Perfect, but with the auxiliary verb in the Conditional

Future Auxiliary Verbs

Avere Essere
Io Avrei Sarei
Tu Avresti Saresti
Lui/Lei Avrebbe Sarebbe
Noi Avremmo Saremmo
Voi Avreste Sareste
Loro Avrebbero Sarebbero

Imperative

The Imperative is used for giving commands.

Amare Credere Dormire
Tu Ama! Credi! Dormi!
Lui/Lei Ami! Creda! Dorma!
Noi Amiamo! Crediamo! Dormiamo!
Voi Amate! Credete! Dormite!
Loro Amino! Credano! Dormano!

Examples

Credi!
Believe!

Crediamo!
Let's believe!
